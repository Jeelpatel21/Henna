Services & Pricing

Bridal Henna

Bridal henna is typically applied to the bride's hands and feet during a special mehndi ceremony, a pre-wedding celebration filled with music, dance, and family festivities. These are usually elaborate designs that contain the groom's initials and symbolize blessings, good fortune, and the bond between the couple.



Parties

Henna is a popular attraction at parties and celebrations. It provides beautiful body art that lasts one to two weeks and serves as a fun, interactive activity for attendees. Common events it is used for are birthday parties, bachelorette parties, showers, festivals, girls' nights, and corporate events.



Jagua Henna

Jagua henna is made from the juice of the Genipa americana fruit, a tropical plant native to South America. Unlike traditional reddish-brown henna, it has a deep blue-black hue, resembling a tattoo.
